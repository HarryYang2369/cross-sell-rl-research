What you're seeing. Early on, every bar is almost all “benefit of the doubt” — the agent knows nothing, so it explores, and for the first stretch it can even trail the random-offer line while it gathers data. As offers play out, the solid “expected value” takes over and the doubt shrinks: it has learned, and now exploits what works — the agent's line pulls off the random floor and climbs toward the best-possible ceiling. That whole arc, cold start to champion, is reinforcement learning in action. These customers and outcomes are simulated, so the numbers show that the method learns — not yet how real customers will behave.
