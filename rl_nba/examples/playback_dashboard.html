What you're seeing. Early on, every bar is almost all “benefit of the doubt” — the agent knows nothing, so it explores. As offers play out, the solid “expected value” takes over and the doubt shrinks: it has learned, and now exploits what works. That shift — and the agent's line climbing from the random floor toward the best-possible ceiling — is reinforcement learning in action. These customers and outcomes are simulated, so the numbers show that the method learns — not yet how real customers will behave.
